Policy Factors

The items we considered while developing our policy.

Economy

Vietnam's GDP per capita growth has been among the fastest in the world over the last 30 years. Accelerating this growth are the three main sectors of the Vietnamese economic system: oil and gas, technology, and manufacturing.As the Vietnamese market continues to steadily expand into the 21st Century, the United States must be at the forefront of these economic advantages.

Labor

These human rights abuses are manifested through the mistreatment of the Vietnamese labor force. Without the ability to assemble or protest, labor costs in Vietnam are particularly low even for Southeast Asian countries. As of 2013, the average monthly wage in Vietnam was just $197, compared to $613 for China. Improvement of labor conditions is also a win for the American markets.

Government

Vietnam is currently governed by the oppressive Communist Party of Vietnam. In direct violation of Article 25 of the International Covenant on Civil Rights, which Vietnam signed in 1982, the Communist Party suppresses opposition by preventing official organization or the contesting of elections. The lack of political freedom and religious freedom is one of the worst human rights violations by Vietnam.
